The SPARK Methodology

Pattern recognition
for people at a threshold.

SPARK isn't a coaching framework. It's a diagnostic lens developed over 40 years on the court, in the locker room, and on the fairway. It finds the pattern running underneath your decisions — the one that shows up in how you grip a golf club, how you open a difficult conversation, and how you've structured your business.

The Diagnostic
Pattern Recognition
Every founder has a pattern. It's the thread that runs through how they make decisions, how they handle pressure, how they treat their people, and how they've built their company. Most can't see it — because they're inside it. Pattern recognition is the ability to stand outside someone's story long enough to name the through-line they've been living.
Why the Golf Course
The Unguarded Reveal
Under mild, unguarded pressure — a bad lie, a crucial putt, a hole that isn't going well — the mask comes off. How you grip a 7-iron under stress is how you grip a partnership decision. How you talk to yourself after a slice is how you talk to yourself after a hard quarter. The course doesn't care about your title. It just shows you the pattern.

The Story Behind the Work

Forty years in locker rooms
taught me something therapy school never does.

My business runs on conversations. When people experience me in a room, things shift for them. That's been true for 40 years of coaching and every client I've ever had. But for most of those years, that truth stayed in locker rooms and on golf courses — places where the social contract allows you to be direct, to ask the uncomfortable question, to hold a mirror steady while someone looks at themselves.

I've been a head golf coach for most of my adult life. And long before "executive coaching" was a category on LinkedIn, I was doing it on the fairway — watching how a CEO grips his 7-iron in the same white-knuckled way he grips a partnership decision. Watching how a founder pre-autopsies a shot before she takes it, the same way she pre-apologises before every difficult conversation.

"How you do one thing is how you do everything. On the course, I can see it in your swing before you can hide it."

The insight that built everything I do came slowly, over thousands of rounds with thousands of athletes. The technical issue is never the real issue. The slice isn't about the club path — it's about the tension that arrived three holes ago and never left. The bad business decision isn't about the market — it's about the pattern that's been running since the company was founded.

Over four decades, I've coached 1,500 athletes and leaders. Purpose Factor® certified. EMCC accredited. But the methodology that actually works — the one that cracked open a Brandywine M&A partner and a Chicago-based CEO in a single hour — came from watching human beings under the mild, unguarded pressure of a round of golf.

The SPARK methodology formalized what I'd been doing intuitively for years. The golf course became the diagnostic tool it always was. And the founders who found me — people like Frank Michel, Jhana Li, Michael Liebowitz — found that in one conversation, I could name the pattern they'd been circling for years.

I'm building something longer-term with Brandon Fong — a platform anchored in the athletics lens, for the leaders who want to work at the intersection of sport and performance. But the foundation for everything is the same: conversations that lead to trust, that lead to people experiencing the work.

Everything starts with getting into more rooms. That's still the needle after 40 years.
