Chapter 06 · LinkedIn Authority Blueprint

Your Inbound Machine

How to turn LinkedIn into your #1 client channel — without it feeling like work.

The Opportunity

Your ideal client is on LinkedIn every morning.

The founder who's outgrown their definition of success. The M&A partner circling a life decision. The CEO whose white-knuckle grip is showing up in every meeting. They're scrolling LinkedIn at 7am while half-listening to their kids at breakfast. They're looking for the thing that stops them — the story, the insight, the one line that makes them feel seen.

Your stories are that thing. The CEO and the 7-iron. Paul's burnout. The two-round experiment. These aren't coaching anecdotes — they're content that converts. LinkedIn is simply the distribution channel you haven't turned on yet.

Content Strategy

The stories that stop the scroll

📖
The Pattern Story (2x/week)
A specific moment from a coaching session, a round of golf, or 40 years of experience that illustrates your core insight. Always starts with a scene. Always ends with the pattern revealed. Never names clients — describes the universal human situation.
Example: "I played a round last week with a CEO who couldn't stop autopsying his shots. By the 9th hole, I realised he did the same thing in every board meeting. Here's what I told him..."
💡
The Conviction Post (1x/week)
A short, direct take on leadership, performance, or decision-making. No fluff. 3-5 sentences. The kind of thing someone screenshots and sends to their business partner. Your philosophy in its purest form.
Example: "Most founders don't have a business problem. They have a grip problem. Squeeze tighter and you lose both the shot and the company. The answer is always: loosen the grip."
🏌️
The Golf Mirror Post (1x/week)
Golf is your unfair advantage. No one else is using the golf course as a business coaching metaphor with this level of specificity and earned experience. Own this lane entirely. Every round you play is content.
Example: "Shot an 83 on Sunday. Back was tweaked — had no choice but to swing within myself. Loosened the grip. Let the game come to me. Shot 4 birdies on the front. Same clubs. Same course. Same player. Different relationship with control."

The Room-Getting Strategy

The 200 List.
Not followers. Real people.

You said it clearly: your pool doesn't need to be big. Maybe 200 people. But they need to be real. This is a fundamentally different strategy from building an audience — and it's the right one for where you are.

The 200 List is a curated, living document of the exact people you want in your world: founders at threshold moments, M&A advisors, EO/YPO chapter leaders, sports executives, high-performance coaches, investors who play golf. People with the mindset and means to engage. Not 10,000 LinkedIn followers. 200 people who matter.

How to Build the 200 List
Step 1 — Mine what you already have. Go back through every SPARK Session client, every speaking engagement attendee, every newsletter subscriber, every LinkedIn connection who has ever engaged with your content. Anyone you'd want to have a conversation with goes on the list. Start here — this alone is probably 50–80 people.

Step 2 — Add the people you haven't met yet. Use LinkedIn's search to find founders in your target profile: $5M–$50M businesses, based in Chicago and surrounding markets, EO/YPO members, golf-adjacent. You're not mass-connecting. You're identifying 10–20 new names per month to add with intention.

Step 3 — Nurture, don't sell. The 200 List is not a sales funnel. It's a relationship ledger. Comment on their content. Send them something relevant (your newsletter, a specific post). Check in on real things. The goal is to be someone they're genuinely glad to hear from — so that when the moment arrives, the conversation is already warm.
The Highest-Leverage Strategy In Your Business

The 15–20 Door Openers.

From your 200-person list, 15–20 people will naturally emerge as door openers — people who put you on a stage, bring you into a meeting, invite you to a roundtable, or say "you need to talk to Bob" in exactly the right room at exactly the right moment.

These people are not clients. They're champions. And activating them is the highest-leverage activity in your entire business — because one door opener can deliver five SPARK Sessions and a Tee Time booking in a single conversation you're not even in the room for.

How to Identify Your Door Openers
Look for people who already exist in the intersection of: (a) they've experienced your work or believe in it deeply, (b) they're well-connected in the rooms where your ideal clients gather, and (c) they're natural connectors — people who get energy from introducing people, not just from being connected.

Past clients who are EO/YPO members. Golf club presidents. Event organisers in the entrepreneurial world. Podcast hosts whose audience is founders. Executive coaches who don't do what you do. People who've referred you before without being asked. Start there.
How to Activate a Door Opener (Message Template)
"[Name] — I wanted to reach out personally. You're one of the people I most respect in this space, and frankly, you've been in my corner in ways you may not even realise. I'm at a point where I'm being more intentional about getting into the right rooms — and I think you know exactly which rooms those are. Would love 20 minutes to share what I'm building and hear who comes to mind for you. No agenda beyond that."
What to Give Your Door Openers
Make it effortless for them to refer you. Give them:

1. One sentence they can say in any room: "If you're a founder who's outgrown the answers you already have, you need to talk to Bob Regnerus."

2. A link to your SPARK Session page — so when someone asks "how do I book him?" they can answer immediately.

3. A complimentary SPARK Session for them if they haven't had one. You cannot activate a door opener who hasn't experienced the work. The session IS the proof of concept.

4. Regular updates on what you're building — door openers stay warm when they feel like insiders, not contacts.
