Where Your Money Is

A full audit of your offers, pricing, and the revenue you're leaving on the table every single month.

Current Offer Audit

Three products. One underutilised.
Two underpriced.

SPARK Session™
Currently $777
One hour. One insight. One action. Strip away the noise, find the pattern, map the next move. Your entry-level offer and most accessible product — currently positioned as a standalone diagnostic for "people circling a real decision."
The issue: $777 is a boutique restaurant price for a boardroom-level intervention. Frank Michel (Brandywine M&A partner), Jhana Li (founder, Spyglass Ops), Caitlin Doemner — these clients have enterprise-level problems and enterprise-level budgets. The price signals the wrong tier. Recommended range: $1,500–$2,500 depending on client profile.
SPARK Tee Time™
$3,400–$4,900
A private, all-inclusive day. Pre-round clarity call, breakfast, 18 holes of coaching in motion, dinner debrief, follow-up SPARK Session, optional plus-1. Black-car service, two nights, two meals. Your most differentiated, most premium, most memorable product.
The issue: Buried in the navigation. No dedicated marketing. No systematic referral pipeline. For a founder spending $5K on a mastermind weekend that gives them worksheets, $4,900 for a pattern reset with a 40-year coach is extraordinary value — they just don't know it exists. This offer needs to lead, not follow.
SPARK Insights™ Newsletter
Free — Unconverted
Your content channel. A recurring touchpoint with your audience, delivering your methodology in digestible form. Existing infrastructure — the hardest part of content marketing (consistency) is theoretically in place.
The issue: A newsletter without a conversion path is a kindness project. Every edition needs a clear thread: read this → feel seen → book this. Currently the newsletter is delivering value without creating action. Chapter 07 addresses this fully.

Missing Revenue Stream

The offer you don't have yet

The Ongoing Engagement

Right now, every client relationship ends at the transaction. SPARK Session → goodbye. Tee Time → goodbye. But your best clients — the M&A partner, the Spyglass founder, the CEO with the white-knuckle grip — don't solve their patterns in a day. They need a mirror on a recurring basis.

A monthly or quarterly retainer engagement ($2,500–$5,000/month) would capture the ongoing value you're currently gifting for free in follow-up conversations, and create predictable recurring revenue that the session model can't provide.

Revenue Leak Summary

What leaves every month without a system

Past client follow-up: Every person who has had a SPARK Session is a warm lead for Tee Time. Without a systematic touchpoint sequence, these relationships cool and the upgrade never happens.

Newsletter-to-booking gap: Subscribers who enjoy your content but have no clear prompt to act are reading your best thinking and paying you nothing. A simple conversion architecture — detailed in Chapter 07 — closes this.

Referral activation: Your testimonials come from credible, well-networked people. Jim Spaulding (Founder-CEO, Philadelphia). Michael Liebowitz (Founder, Magnetic Mind Studio). These people know other founders. A structured referral ask — at the right moment — turns clients into a distribution channel.

Speaking revenue: Your speaking and media page exists but isn't positioned as a revenue line. A keynote on "how you do one thing is how you do everything" lands powerfully for executive audiences. $5K–$25K per engagement is realistic at your level.
