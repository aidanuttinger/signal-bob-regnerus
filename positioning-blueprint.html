The Coach Reg Signal

Who you are, who you're for, and how to say it so the right people immediately feel seen — and reach out.

The Problem With "Coach"

Everyone is a coach. No one is you.

The word "coach" has been so thoroughly diluted it now means almost nothing. A 25-year-old with a certification and a podcast is a coach. A former burnout survivor with a framework is a coach. You — 40 years in locker rooms and boardrooms, 1,500 athletes, a Purpose Factor® facilitator, EMCC-accredited, with a track record that speaks for itself — you are also called a coach.

The positioning problem isn't your title. It's that your current positioning doesn't communicate the gap between you and the noise. Your ideal client — the founder who has already hired coaches and outgrown them — needs to immediately sense that you're different. The current framing doesn't create that sensation fast enough.

You don't coach people. You show them the pattern running through everything they do — and then you help them change it.
The Repositioning
Recommended Positioning Statement
For founders and leaders who've already done the work —
and still can't see the pattern.
Bob Regnerus (Coach Reg) is a pattern recognition coach for high performers at threshold moments. After 40 years and 1,500 athletes, he sees what most people spend years in therapy trying to find — and he finds it on a golf course, in a conversation, in the way you grip a club.

This positioning does several things the current framing doesn't. It immediately signals a sophisticated audience (founders who've "already done the work"). It creates intrigue around pattern recognition. It connects the golf methodology to the business methodology in one sentence. And it makes "Coach Reg" — the brand — feel earned rather than assumed.

Who You're For vs. Not For

The right people. Precisely defined.

✓ Your People

Founders and leaders at a threshold — outgrown their definition of success, circling a real decision, exhausted by achievement that no longer satisfies. Have done therapy, masterminds, other coaches. Intellectually sophisticated. Want a mirror, not a prescription. Deal sizes in their world: $50K+. Golf optional but advantageous.

✗ Not Your People

Early-stage entrepreneurs looking for business tactics. People who want to be told what to do. "General growth" seekers with no specific threshold. Anyone who needs motivation rather than conviction. People who aren't ready to act on what they discover. The SPARK Session page already says this — now the positioning needs to say it upfront.

Core Messaging Architecture

The words that convert

Hero Statement (Website / LinkedIn Headline)
"I help founders and leaders who've outgrown the answers they already have."
Why it works: Immediately signals a sophisticated audience. Creates curiosity. Implies depth beyond tactics. The people it describes feel immediately seen — and that's the fastest path to "I need to talk to this person."
The One-Liner (Elevator / Social Bio)
"40 years. 1,500 athletes. One question: what's the pattern running through everything you do?"
Why it works: The numbers create immediate authority. The question creates immediate engagement. It's personal without being vague, specific without being limiting.
The Philosophy Line (Content / Speaking Opening)
"How you do one thing is how you do everything. On the course, I can see it in your swing before you can hide it."
Why it works: This is your intellectual property. Your proprietary insight. "As within, so without" is ancient wisdom made specific and modern through golf. Lead with this — it's distinctive, quotable, and positions you as a thinker, not just a practitioner.
The Invitation (Call to Action)
"Bring me your $50K problem. In one hour, we'll find the pattern underneath it."
Why it works: Specific dollar amount qualifies the prospect immediately. "Pattern underneath it" is intriguing and specific to your methodology. It's confident without being arrogant — it's a diagnostic invitation, not a sales pitch.
Brand Voice

How Coach Reg sounds

The Voice Is

Direct. Warm. A little laconic. Earned authority that doesn't need to announce itself. Honest about imperfection (the 107 round is as important as the 83). Story-first — always a specific moment, not a generality. The kind of person who buys you a drink at the turn and says exactly the right thing.

The Voice Is Not

Hype. Hustle. "10x your revenue in 90 days." Jargon-heavy coaching speak. Overly philosophical without grounding. Performative vulnerability. The voice never shows off — it shows up. Quietly. Precisely. With the one question that cracks everything open.
