Question 01 of 05

Where are you most stuck right now?

Don't overthink it. Your first instinct is usually your truest answer.

A
I can't let go of control
I know I need to delegate, step back, or trust the process — but I can't seem to do it. The tighter I hold, the worse things get.
B
I'm invisible — and my work isn't
My expertise is real but no one knows I exist. I'm not showing up in the market the way I should. The broadcast doesn't match the work.
C
I keep circling the same decision
I know what I need to do. I've known for a while. And yet I haven't done it. There's something stopping me that isn't logic.
D
The same problem keeps arriving in new forms
New team, same dynamic. New market, same result. I've solved this before — and somehow it's back. Different wrapper, same pattern.

A
Less than 6 months

B
6 months to 2 years
It's been there long enough that I've tried to work around it. Some of those attempts helped. Most didn't stick.
C

This has been a thread in my life and business for years. I've been aware of it, tried to address it, and it persists.
D

This is foundational. It's in my story. It's been running long enough that it feels like part of who I am — not something I'm doing.
Question 03 of 05

What's the real cost
in your business right now?

Think about what this pattern has cost you — in revenue, in time, in decisions you didn't make.

A
Missed opportunities — but I'm not sure the dollar amount
I know something is being left on the table. I just haven't quantified it. The cost feels more emotional than financial right now.
B
$5K–$25K per month in stalled or lost revenue
I can see specific deals, clients, or launches that didn't happen because of this pattern. The math is real.
C
$25K–$75K per month in unrealized potential
My business should be at a significantly different level. The gap between where I am and where I know I could be is substantial.
D
$75K+ per month — this is a multi-million dollar pattern
I'm operating well below my ceiling. The compounded cost of this pattern over years is the kind of number that keeps me up at night.
Question 04 of 05

What have you already
tried?

Understanding what hasn't worked is as important as understanding what the problem is.

A
Strategy and planning — courses, consultants, frameworks
I've optimized the tactics. The strategy is sound. The pattern is somewhere deeper than the plan.
B
Coaching or therapy — personal development work
I've done the inner work. I have self-awareness. And yet the pattern persists. The insight isn't the problem — the application is.
C
Masterminds, peer groups, high-level networks
I've been in the rooms with the right people. I've heard the advice. I've implemented some of it. The pattern is still running.
D
All of the above — and more than once
I've tried everything the market offers for high performers. Some of it helped. None of it addressed the root pattern. That's why I'm here.

Not just in the business. In how you feel when you wake up in the morning.

A

C

D

I've grown. The business hasn't kept up. When the pattern breaks, there's finally alignment between who I am and what I'm building.
