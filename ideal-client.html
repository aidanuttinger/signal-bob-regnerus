Chapter 08 · Ideal Client Profile

The Person You Do Your Best
Work With

Defined precisely — so every message, offer, and conversation attracts them and repels everyone else.

Why This Matters

The clearer you are about who you're for, the more powerfully the right people self-select. Vague positioning attracts vague clients — people who want general motivation, who aren't ready to act, who don't have a specific real decision in front of them. Precise positioning attracts the Frank Michels and Jhana Lis — credible, committed, and ready to move.

Your best clients have already told you exactly who they are. Their testimonials are the brief.

Primary ICP — The Threshold Founder
The Threshold Founder
Your core client · SPARK Sessions + Tee Time · Highest LTV
Who They Are
Founder or CEO, 10–30 years in. Built something real. Revenue between $2M–$50M. Respected externally. Internally questioning everything.
Where They Are
At a threshold — a decision, a transition, a chapter that needs to end. They've outgrown their definition of success but haven't found the next one yet.
What They've Tried
Therapy. Other coaches. Masterminds. Executive retreats. They're not looking for information — they've had plenty. They're looking for someone who can see what they can't.
Their Investment Mindset
$1,500 for a SPARK Session is not a decision for this person. $5,500 for Tee Time requires a conversation but not a CFO review. They value results over price.
"I've worked with other coaches before, but Bob is truly one of the best I've ever had the pleasure of working with." — Michael Liebowitz, Founder, Magnetic Mind Studio
They Use Words Like
  • "I feel stuck but I don't know why"
  • "What got me here isn't working anymore"
  • "I've outgrown this version of myself"
  • "Something feels off and I can't articulate it"
  • "I know the answer — I just can't see it"
Where To Find Them
  • LinkedIn (founder/CEO titles, 10+ years experience)
  • EO, YPO, Vistage chapters
  • M&A and wealth management networks
  • Speaking at founder conferences
  • Referred by clients like Frank Michel, Jhana Li
Secondary ICP — The High-Performance Leader
The High-Performance Leader
Athletes, executives, coaches · SPARK Sessions · Pipeline to Tee Time
Who They Are
Athletes transitioning from sport to business or leadership. Coaches moving from the court to the boardroom. Executives who grew up as athletes and still think in performance terms.
Their Situation
Accustomed to performance feedback. Comfortable with coaching relationships. Not threatened by the process — but needs someone with enough sport credibility to command respect.
Why They Choose Bob
40 years. 1,500 athletes. Head golf coach today. You're not a business person cosplaying as a sports coach — you're the real thing, and they can feel it immediately.
Conversion Path
SPARK Session → speaking referral → Tee Time. High potential for peer-to-peer referrals within sports/executive networks.
Anti-Profile — Who Is Not Your Client
Not Your People — Be Clear About This

The information seeker — wants frameworks and strategies, not a mirror. Will debate your insights instead of sitting with them. Takes notes but doesn't act.  ·  The early-stage founder — needs tactics, not patterns. The threshold work you do requires some accrued experience to land.  ·  The permission-seeker — wants you to tell them it's okay to keep circling. Your page already says "if you're looking for permission to keep circling, I'm not your guy" — that's right.  ·  The price-objector — if $1,500 is a stretch, the work won't land at the depth required. Protect the container.
