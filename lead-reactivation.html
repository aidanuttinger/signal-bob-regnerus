Chapter 05 · Lead Reactivation

The Warm List Playbook

Re-engaging your dormant network — newsletter subscribers, past clients, lost referrals — and turning past conversations into booked sessions.

The Opportunity

Your warmest leads are already waiting

Every person who has ever had a SPARK Session, attended a talk, subscribed to your newsletter, sent a referral, or asked "can we connect sometime?" is a warm lead. They already know you. They've already experienced or been adjacent to your work. The reason they haven't booked again isn't that they don't value what you do — it's that no one asked them to.

Reactivation isn't selling to strangers. It's reminding people who already believe in you that you're available.

Segment Your Warm List

Three pools. Three conversations.

Pool 1: Past SPARK Session Clients
Everyone who has booked a SPARK Session is a warm candidate for Tee Time. They've experienced your methodology. They know what a breakthrough with you feels like. The conversion rate from Session → Tee Time will be your highest of any pipeline. These people need one specific message: "The next level of what you experienced is available."
Pool 2: Newsletter Subscribers Who Haven't Booked
These people opted in, which means they saw something they connected with. They're reading your thinking (or they were). They need a reason to take the next step. Not a discount — an invitation. A direct, personal message that says: "You've been reading my work. Here's what working together looks like."
Pool 3: Past Referrers & Near-Misses
People who referred someone, or who got close to booking and went quiet. The referrers should hear "the person you sent me had a breakthrough — thank you — and I'd love to offer you the same." The near-misses need a different follow-up: not a push, but a genuine check-in that removes the barrier that stopped them.
The Reactivation Sequence

What to say and when

1
Day 1 · Personal Touch
The No-Pressure Check-In
A brief, personal message — not a newsletter, not a broadcast. Just you, to them. Reference something specific about their situation or your last interaction. No ask. Just presence.
2
Day 4 · Value Drop
Send Something Genuinely Useful
A piece of your thinking — a specific insight, story, or framework — that's directly relevant to where they are. Not a sales email. A gift. The kind of thing that makes someone say "this is exactly what I needed right now."
3
Day 8 · The Invitation
One Clear Ask
One specific, low-friction invitation. Not "let me know if you ever want to work together." Something like: "I have two SPARK Sessions open in the next two weeks. If you're circling something real, bring it to me." Specific. Bounded. Easy to say yes to.
4
Day 14 · The Close or Release
Final Touch — With Dignity
If no response: a final short note. "Totally understand if the timing's off. I'll be here when it's right." This closes the loop without burning the relationship — and often gets a reply precisely because it removes pressure.
Message Templates

Words that don't feel like marketing

For Past SPARK Session Clients
"[Name] — I've been thinking about you. We worked through [specific thing] a while back. I'm curious how it's landed since then. No agenda, just checking in. How are you?"
The Tee Time Upgrade Message
"[Name] — I've been offering something new this year that I think you'd connect with deeply. It's a full day — golf course, breakfast, dinner, the works — where we go much further than an hour allows. I've got two slots in the spring. Would you be open to a quick conversation about it?"
For Newsletter Subscribers (Conversion)
"You've been on my list for a while — which means you've seen my thinking. I want to offer you something beyond the newsletter. I have a handful of SPARK Session slots available this month. If you're circling a real decision — business, career, life — bring it to me. One hour. One insight. One action. Reply here and we'll find a time."
For Referrers
"[Name] — I want you to know that [person you referred] had a real breakthrough. Your instinct to connect us was generous. I'd love to offer you the same. When's a good time to talk?"
